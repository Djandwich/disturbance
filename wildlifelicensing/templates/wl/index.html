    Welcome to Wildlife Licensing

    The Department of Parks and Wildlife can issue licences under the Wildlife Conservation Act 1950, the Wildlife Conservation Regulations 1970 and the Wildlife Conservation Regulations (Reptiles and Amphibians) 2003 for a range of activities involving flora (plants, fungi and algae) or fauna (animals except fish). We also issue Restricted Keeping Permits for birds on behalf of the Department of Agriculture and Food under the Biosecurity and Agriculture Management Act 2007.

    This web portal allows you to apply for a licence, renew an existing licence and provide returns information.

    Simply set up a profile, select the licence type you wish to apply for and get started.

    If you wish to do any of the following activities, you may need a licence.

    FLORA LICENCES

    • ‘Take’ flora (includes gather, pluck, cut, pull up, destroy, dig up, remove or injure plants or any part of a plant) from Crown land for:
      • commercial purposes (commercial purposes licence)
      • scientific/non-commercial purposes (scientific or other prescribed purposes licence)
    • Sell flora taken from private land (commercial producers licence)

    FAUNA LICENCES

    • ‘Take’ fauna (includes hunt, kill, capture, disturb or molest by any means mammals, reptiles, birds, amphibians or any part of an animal) for any purpose, including:
      • mitigate damage to property being caused by fauna (damage licence)
      • remove or destroy dangerous fauna (dangerous fauna licence)
      • take kangaroos for sale (professional shooter’s licence)
      • educational or public purposes (regulation 15 licence)
      • scientific purposes (scientific regulation 17 licence)
      • band birds (bird banding licence)
      • taking of emu eggs by persons of Aboriginal descent for carving or cultural purposes
    • Process fauna carcasses (processing licence)
    • Deal in fauna carcasses (direct dealer’s licence)
    • Deal in fauna skins (skin dealers licence)

    BIRD LICENCES

    • Keep and breed native birds as pets (avicultural licence)
    • Keep certain introduced/exotic birds as pets (restricted keeping permit)
    • Buy and sell or otherwise deal in birds (bird dealers licence)
    • Keep and breed fauna other than birds (e.g. kangaroos and emus) (wildlife licence)
    • Farm and breed fauna for sale and commercial display (fauna farm licence)
    • Keep fauna for educational and public purposes (regulation 16 or wildlife park licence)

    REPTILE LICENCES

    • Keep reptiles and amphibians like snakes, lizards, turtles and frogs (herpetofauna) as pets (herpetofauna keeper’s licence)
    • Deal in pet herpetofauna (herpetofauna dealer’s licence)
    • Take pet herpetofauna from the wild for sale (herpetofauna taker’s licence)
    • Farm, breed, keep and sell pet herpetofauna (herpetofauna farmer’s licence)
    • Bring pet herpetofauna into Western Australia (herpetofauna import licence)
    • Take pet herpetofauna out of Western Australia (herpetofauna export licence)
    • Remove venomous/dangerous and non-venomous reptiles from in or near residential or other premises frequented by people (reptile removalist’s licence)

    IMPORT/EXPORT LICENCES

    • Take fauna out of Western Australia (export licence)
    • Bring fauna into Western Australia (import licence)
    • Import live exotic birds and other animals (exotic animal import licence)

    CALM ACT LANDS AND WATERS

    If you are intending to undertake activities on any lands or waters managed by the department under the Conservation and Land Management Act 1984 (CALM Act land and waters), then you may need a lawful authority to do so. CALM Act land and waters includes nature reserves, national parks, conservation parks, marine nature reserves, marine parks, marine management areas, State forest and timber reserves, and a number of miscellaneous reserves.
